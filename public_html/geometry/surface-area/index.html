Surface area

In Depth

Surface area measures the total area of the outer surface of a three-dimensional solid. For polyhedra, it is the sum of the areas of all faces. For curved surfaces, it requires integration.

Key formulas: sphere \(SA=4\pi r^2\), cylinder (lateral) \(SA=2\pi rh\), cone (lateral) \(SA=\pi r l\) where \(l=\sqrt{r^2+h^2}\) is the slant height. These are derived by 'unrolling' the surface into a flat shape.

For a surface of revolution generated by rotating \(y=f(x)\) about the \(x\)-axis: \(SA=2\pi\int_a^b f(x)\sqrt{1+[f'(x)]^2}\,dx\). The factor \(\sqrt{1+[f'(x)]^2}\) accounts for the arc length element.

The isoperimetric inequality in 3D: among all surfaces enclosing a fixed volume \(V\), the sphere minimizes surface area \(SA=4\pi r^2\) where \(V=\frac{4}{3}\pi r^3\). This explains why soap bubbles are spherical — surface tension minimizes surface area for a given enclosed volume.

Surface area is critical in engineering and biology. Heat transfer rate is proportional to surface area (Newton's law of cooling). The surface-to-volume ratio \(SA/V\) decreases as objects scale up, limiting cell size and explaining why small animals lose heat faster relative to their mass.

Key Properties & Applications

Surface area calculations are essential in engineering and science. Heat exchangers maximize surface area to improve thermal transfer. Catalysts use porous materials with enormous surface areas (activated carbon: up to 3000 m²/g). Drug delivery systems use surface area to control dissolution rates.

For parametric surfaces \(\mathbf{r}(u,v)\), the surface area element is \(dS=|\mathbf{r}_u\times\mathbf{r}_v|\,du\,dv\). The cross product of the partial derivatives gives the normal vector; its magnitude is the area scaling factor. This formula unifies all surface area calculations.

The isoperimetric inequality in 3D: \(SA^3\geq36\pi V^2\), with equality only for the sphere. This means the sphere is the most efficient shape for enclosing volume — a fact exploited by cells, bubbles, and pressure vessels.
